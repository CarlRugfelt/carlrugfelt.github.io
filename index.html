About me

I've spent over a decade working with sustainability in large, complex organisations. Before that, I spent years in the field — mapping nature reserves, assessing environmental impact, learning to read a landscape and reading companies. I'm not easily rattled. I don't have a hidden agenda. I tend to see possibility where others see obstacles — not naively, but because I've seen what happens when someone decides to actually try.

My background is in sustainability reporting and GHG accounting at the highest level. But the work I find most meaningful is what happens around the table: building understanding, finding common ground, and helping organisations take the next step they didn't think they were ready for.

That's the kind of work I'm looking for more of.

That combination shapes how I work. I understand the science. I understand the data. And I've learned, through enough stakeholder meetings and cross-functional projects, that none of it moves unless the people in the room want it to.

My strength lies in moving between the technical and the strategic — from building automated data collection workflows to presenting decision support for management and boards.
